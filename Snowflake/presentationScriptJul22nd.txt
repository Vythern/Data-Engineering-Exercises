The pipeline of our project begins with a few raw datasets in the form of CSVs.  
Before we make use of them though, we need to setup our tables- 
We configure spark to use Apache Iceberg, with a glue catalog for metadata, 
and we also set our driver to communicate with a specific warehouse location.  
In this case, our s3 bucket, and prepare reading and writing for our iceberg table with or into it.  


We begin the transformation portion of ETL with Spark, filtering the data.  
There are numerous operations we can perform to clean it, with a few examples here:  
We might replace null loyalty points of a customer with 0 by default, or alternatively-
-if the data is crucial, simply drop the records that are null.  

Then, with our cleaned dataframes, we can load up our S3 with the real table data.  
AWS Glue, designed the way that it is (for interoperability), acts as a central metadata catalog; 
It lets other tools discover and access these tables without cloning the data.  We read directly from the S3, such as 
is the case with our snowflake analysis program.  Snowflake is able to access this data, and from there, we can perform our queries and analysis.  
As you can see here, we can specify the external volume (our S3), a catalog, (glue, which describes our schema and where to find the data)-
-and the name of the iceberg table.  Now we are ready to query it.
